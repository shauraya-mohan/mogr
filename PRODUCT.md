# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Men, roughly 16–35, who want to look and feel their best but find grooming advice
scattered, generic, or intimidating. They're online-native and social-media-aware.
They use mogr to scan themselves once and get clear, confidence-building direction
across skin, hair, facial hair, and wardrobe. The core surface is the logged-in
console (dashboard, scans, routine, wardrobe, per-feature results); a marketing
landing page exists but the product IS the console.

## Product Purpose

Turn a single selfie plus light questionnaire input into personalized, encouraging
grooming coaching and face-preserving previews, all building one profile that gets
richer with every scan (face shape, skin type/undertone, hair, beard, wardrobe).
Success = a man completes a scan workflow, trusts the read, acts on the routine,
and returns. It is a grooming coach, not a dermatologist or a rating app.

## Brand Personality

Confident, encouraging, precise, premium, masculine — "barber × tech," no-nonsense.
Voice leads with a genuine strength, frames improvements as upgrades (never flaws),
and is qualitative, never quantitative (no scores, rankings, or percentiles).
Microcopy is lowercase and unfussy; labels and metadata are uppercase mono.
Emotionally: the user should close a screen feeling capable and energized, not
self-conscious.

## Anti-references

- Score-based "looksmaxxing" / face-rating apps (harsh numbers, percentile shame).
- Templated AI-generated product UI: the "Lovable / Replit / SaaS-cream" look —
  generic rounded-square icon chips, thin lucide line icons in tinted boxes,
  centered form cards marooned in empty space, sparkle/plus filler icons, bland
  "Loading…" text. This is the explicit thing to avoid; every surface should read
  as deliberately designed by a studio, not scaffolded.
- Clinical/medical dashboards; loud neon fintech; anything that reads cold.

## Design Principles

1. **Strengths first, always.** Every result opens with something already working;
   improvements are framed as upgrades. Copy is design material.
2. **Qualitative, never a number.** No scores, grades, or percentiles anywhere.
3. **One bronze moment per view.** Bronze is the single accent, used sparingly;
   the page is predominantly ink/bone/cloud. Spend boldness in one place.
4. **Editorial restraint, executed precisely.** Minimal direction means spacing,
   type, and detail must be exact. Reuse the brand devices (the "." dot, comb-tooth
   monoline, capture reticle) so surfaces feel cohesive and intentional.
5. **Distinctive, not templated.** If an interface could be guessed as "AI-made"
   from a glance, it fails. Prefer characterful, meaningful marks over stock icons.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` on every animation (loaders, reveals, sweeps).
- Body/interactive text meets WCAG AA contrast on ink/bone/cloud; avoid washed grey
  body text on tinted grounds.
- Visible keyboard focus; controls named by what they do.
- Dark mode is a designed theme (default), not an inversion.
